# Wisdom Engine Principles

## Purpose

LifeGoalApp is designed as more than a productivity tracker. The product direction is a **wisdom engine**: a system that helps people grow through repeated small perspective shifts over time.

Core thesis:

- People do not usually transform from reading long philosophy explanations once.
- Wisdom compounds through many short, lived experiences and reflections.
- The app should deliver tiny moments of insight inside daily loops users already revisit.

---

## 1) Core product principle: micro-insights, not lectures

Design for short, high-frequency interactions:

- 30–90 second reflections
- brief questions
- small mental experiments
- moments of surprise or reframe

Every interaction should create a small shift. Over months, these shifts accumulate into stronger judgment, flexibility, and self-awareness.

---

## 2) Discovery over instruction

Users learn better when they *discover* ideas rather than being told what to think.

Product rule:

- Prefer prompts that help users notice patterns in their own life.
- Use examples and choices that reveal change, trade-offs, and uncertainty.
- Avoid preachy or absolute framing.

This aligns with the AI coach model of reflective questioning and non-dogmatic guidance.

---

## 3) Mental models library (toolbox approach)

Treat wisdom as a trainable set of mental tools.

Suggested model areas:

- cognitive biases
- identity formation and identity flexibility
- meaning creation
- emotional regulation
- uncertainty tolerance
- perspective-taking and empathy

Delivery format for each model:

1. short story or scenario
2. tiny exercise
3. practical “use this today” action

The goal is applied thinking, not abstract theory.

---

## 4) Personal insight tracking

Add explicit support for “insight moments,” not only tasks completed.

Track entries such as:

- “I realized something today”
- “I changed my mind about …”
- “My confidence changed from X% to Y%”

Over time, users should be able to see their thinking evolve.

---

## 5) AI as a Socratic guide

The AI coach should act as a guide, not a lecturer.

Operating pattern:

- ask short, relevant questions
- probe assumptions gently
- surface contradictions without shaming
- help users choose small next actions

This reinforces autonomy while still increasing clarity and self-correction.

---

## 6) Gamify perspective shifts

Gamification should reward growth in thinking, not only output volume.

Examples of rewardable events:

- recognizing a bias
- revising confidence after evidence
- exploring an unfamiliar perspective
- completing a rebalance action across life axes

This creates “wisdom XP” alongside productivity XP.

---

## 7) Social wisdom exchange (optional, safe)

Support anonymous, consent-based sharing of insights and reflections.

Desired outcomes:

- exposure to diverse perspectives
- increased humility
- stronger empathy for different life experiences

Guardrails:

- privacy-first controls
- clear moderation policy
- anti-harassment and anti-dogmatism standards

---

## 8) Long-term product effect

If users engage for a few minutes daily over long periods, they can accumulate hundreds of small perspective shifts.

Expected changes over time:

- better interpretation of experience
- improved uncertainty handling
- stronger perspective-taking
- more adaptive meaning-making

---

## 9) Real success metrics

The primary success outcome is not “turn users into philosophers.”

A realistic success profile is users becoming:

- more reflective
- less dogmatic
- more curious
- better at uncertainty
- more empathetic across viewpoints

Track these as behavioral indicators through lightweight in-app signals.

---

## 10) Integration with existing LifeGoalApp loops

Apply these principles inside loops users already revisit:

- today habits and routines
- journal flows
- life wheel check-ins
- dashboard/micro-quests
- AI coaching interactions

Principle: inject wisdom moments into existing behavior loops before adding large new surfaces.

---

## Implementation guidance (phased)

### Phase A — Low-friction insertion
- Add one micro-insight card per day to existing loop surfaces.
- Add one “change-in-confidence” interaction to reflection/journal flow.
- Add lightweight event tracking for perspective-shift actions.

### Phase B — Structured model tracks
- Ship a small starter mental-model set (3–5 models).
- Attach each model to a story + exercise + action format.
- Connect rewards to completed reflection actions.

### Phase C — Social and advanced guidance
- Introduce anonymous insight sharing (opt-in).
- Add moderation tooling and privacy controls.
- Expand AI-coach follow-up patterns for deeper Socratic dialogue.

---

## Product language guardrails

To keep the wisdom engine healthy and trustworthy:

- do not claim certainty or final truth
- do not moralize or shame
- do not reward extremism
- do reward curiosity, correction, and balance
- do keep the experience practical, short, and repeatable
