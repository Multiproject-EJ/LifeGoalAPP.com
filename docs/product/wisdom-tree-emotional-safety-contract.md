# Wisdom Tree Emotional Safety Contract

## Purpose

This contract defines the emotional safety, trust, and ethical boundaries for the Wisdom Tree system before Phase 1 implementation.

Future Wisdom Tree work must preserve the feeling of a cozy magical card encounter, symbolic reflection moment, gentle companion, and story mirror. It must never become therapy, diagnosis, manipulation, moral scoring, productivity correction, emotional surveillance, or an infinite chatbot.

---

## 1. Product identity

### What Wisdom Tree is

Wisdom Tree is:

- A cozy magical card encounter inside the LifeGoalApp world.
- A short symbolic reflection moment.
- A gentle companion that offers language, not answers.
- A story mirror that helps users notice what feels kind, possible, or true today.
- A lightweight pause that users can accept, skip, close, or continue through without pressure.

### What Wisdom Tree is not

Wisdom Tree is not:

- Therapy, coaching, counseling, treatment, or crisis support.
- A diagnostic tool or mental-health assessment.
- A personality test, moral score, productivity score, or life score.
- A system that decides what a user needs.
- A correction engine for habits, emotions, identity, or choices.
- A hidden emotional profile or surveillance layer.
- An open-ended chatbot or endless conversation loop.

---

## 2. Core safety principles

- **User agency:** Users choose whether to engage, save, skip, close, or continue. The experience must never imply that one emotional choice is required.
- **Emotional humility:** Wisdom Tree can offer a possible mirror. It must not claim certainty about the user's feelings, motives, personality, needs, or future.
- **No diagnosis:** Do not label users with mental-health, attachment, personality, trauma, or clinical terms.
- **No shame:** Do not scold users for missed habits, inconsistent progress, difficult emotions, or choosing not to reflect.
- **No moral ranking:** Choices must not imply that one user is wiser, kinder, stronger, or better than another.
- **No reward manipulation:** Emotional answers must not tune dice, essence, odds, economy, or core rewards.
- **No hidden profiling:** Do not infer or store secret emotional types, risk scores, personality scores, or private traits behind the user's back.
- **No forced journaling:** Journaling must be optional. Users must be able to complete or leave the encounter without writing.
- **No infinite AI chat:** Wisdom Tree should be bounded, card-like, and completeable. It must not become an endless assistant conversation.

---

## 3. Forbidden claims

Do not use claims like these in product copy, prompts, code comments, AI instructions, analytics labels, or reviewer-facing text:

- “You are anxious.”
- “You are avoidant.”
- “You are depressed.”
- “You are emotionally blocked.”
- “The app knows what you need.”
- “This choice means you failed.”
- “Your personality score says…”
- “Your attachment style is…”
- “You need to fix this pattern.”
- “You chose the less healthy option.”
- “Your habits show you are not committed.”
- “Wisdom Tree detected your emotional state.”
- “The correct choice is…”
- “Users like you should…”

---

## 4. Allowed language

Preferred language is optional, warm, short, and non-certain:

- “This might fit today.”
- “A small step is still real.”
- “Choose what feels kind.”
- “The tree offers a mirror, not a verdict.”
- “You can continue without saving.”
- “Both paths are welcome.”
- “Take the card that feels lighter.”
- “You can leave this here.”
- “Maybe today asks for softness.”
- “No need to solve everything now.”
- “This is only a tiny reflection.”
- “Skip this if it does not fit.”

---

## 5. AI boundaries for future phases

Phase 1 must not include AI calls. Future phases may explore AI only if these boundaries are preserved:

- AI must be optional.
- A handcrafted fallback must always be available.
- Do not generate live AI content per tile, per roll, or per frequent gameplay event.
- Do not send raw journal history to AI by default.
- Do not use clinical labels, diagnosis-like labels, or personality scoring labels.
- Server-side quotas are required before any app-funded generation.
- Use cached bundles only for generated Wisdom Tree content.
- AI output must be bounded to card-like content, not open-ended chat.
- AI prompts must instruct the model to avoid diagnosis, shame, certainty claims, and moral ranking.
- Users must be able to use Wisdom Tree without AI personalization.

---

## 6. Data and consent boundaries

- Do not store Wisdom Tree data in Island Run runtime state.
- Do not add Wisdom Tree emotional fields to gameplay runtime state.
- Do not store raw journal text in Wisdom Tree memory tables.
- Do not copy raw habit notes, goal notes, or private reflections into Wisdom Tree memory.
- Birthday or life-stage use must be optional and separately consented.
- Prefer user-selected broad life-stage or tone settings over deriving sensitive context.
- Personalization must be explainable: users should understand what signals are used and why.
- Personalization must be resettable.
- Users must be able to turn off analysis and personalization.
- Users must be able to continue without saving reflection content.
- Any future memory must be lightweight, consented, scoped to Wisdom Tree, and removable.

---

## 7. Reward-loop boundaries

- Emotional choices must not change dice, essence, odds, shards, spins, stop tickets, minigame tickets, boss progress, or core rewards.
- Do not frame rewards as proof that the user is a better human.
- Do not create “healthier choice = better reward” mechanics.
- Do not use emotional disclosure to improve economy outcomes.
- Completing a Wisdom Tree encounter can progress the Wisdom stop.
- The selected choice should not morally rank the player.
- Copy must avoid implying that reflection quality determines worth, wisdom, discipline, or future luck.

---

## 8. Copywriting rules

- Use normal language only.
- Avoid academic, clinical, therapeutic, or diagnostic language.
- Keep wording short, cozy, and concrete.
- Both choices should feel valid.
- Never shame missed habits, skipped reflections, slow progress, or inconsistent play.
- Avoid “should.”
- Avoid certainty claims.
- Avoid productivity-correction framing.
- Avoid labels that sound like traits, disorders, types, scores, or diagnoses.
- Prefer “might,” “maybe,” “today,” “could,” “if it helps,” and “you can.”
- Endings should feel complete, not like a hook into an infinite conversation.

## 9. Examples

### Flame

Good:

- “Flame offers a small spark. Choose the warmth you want to carry.”
- “A tiny brave step is enough for today.”
- Choice A: “Try one small spark.”
- Choice B: “Rest beside the fire.”

Bad:

- “You are avoiding action.”
- “Choose courage to prove you are growing.”
- “Your low drive means you need a productivity correction.”
- “Pick the stronger path for a better reward.”

### Hearth

Good:

- “Hearth invites you to come back to what feels safe.”
- “Choose the kind of comfort that fits right now.”
- Choice A: “Make something easier.”
- Choice B: “Let something wait.”

Bad:

- “You are needy.”
- “Your home score is weak.”
- “The app knows you need stability.”
- “Missing routines means you failed your foundation.”

### Tide

Good:

- “Tide moves in and out. You can move gently too.”
- “This might be a day for changing shape.”
- Choice A: “Loosen one plan.”
- Choice B: “Keep one steady shore.”

Bad:

- “You are emotionally unstable.”
- “Your mood pattern says you cannot commit.”
- “The tide detected anxiety.”
- “Choose flexibility or lose momentum.”

### Storm

Good:

- “Storm does not ask you to fix the sky.”
- “You can take shelter before choosing the next step.”
- Choice A: “Pause under cover.”
- Choice B: “Name one sturdy thing.”

Bad:

- “You are overwhelmed.”
- “You are bad at regulation.”
- “Your stress score is high.”
- “Strong players push through storms.”

### Bloom

Good:

- “Bloom notices what is beginning, even if it is small.”
- “A quiet beginning still counts.”
- Choice A: “Water one tiny thing.”
- Choice B: “Let the seed rest.”

Bad:

- “You are not reaching your potential.”
- “Your growth mindset is low.”
- “You failed to bloom this week.”
- “Better people choose growth.”

### Mirror

Good:

- “Mirror offers a glimpse, not a verdict.”
- “Choose what feels honest and kind.”
- Choice A: “Notice one true thing.”
- Choice B: “Leave the mirror cloudy.”

Bad:

- “This reveals who you really are.”
- “Your personality score says you hide from truth.”
- “The app understands your real motive.”
- “Choosing not to answer means avoidance.”

---

## 10. Phase 1 acceptance checklist

A Phase 1 Wisdom Tree PR must pass all of the following:

- [ ] No AI calls.
- [ ] No migrations.
- [ ] No reward or economy changes.
- [ ] No diagnosis-like wording.
- [ ] No forced journaling.
- [ ] No runtime-state emotional fields.
- [ ] Card choices are emotionally safe.
- [ ] Users can close or continue without pressure.
- [ ] Wisdom Tree data is not stored in Island Run runtime state.
- [ ] Emotional choices do not change dice, essence, odds, or core rewards.
- [ ] Both choices feel valid.
- [ ] Copy uses short, normal, cozy language.
- [ ] Copy avoids “should” and certainty claims.
- [ ] The encounter is bounded and does not create an infinite chat loop.
