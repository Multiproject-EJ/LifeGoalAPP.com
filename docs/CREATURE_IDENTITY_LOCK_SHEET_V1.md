# Creature Identity Lock Sheet V1

## Purpose
This sheet locks identity direction for the 8 highest-overlap/highest-risk creatures identified in the Dex audit before final art production. It is docs-only planning guidance and does **not** change gameplay systems, IDs, economy, or schema.

## Scope lock
- Target creatures only:
  - `common-drift-pup`
  - `common-fern-fox`
  - `common-garden-puff`
  - `common-lichen-kit`
  - `rare-mirage-pup`
  - `mythic-celest-pup`
  - `mythic-aurora-maned-cat`
  - `mythic-infinity-sprite`
- Keep current code IDs and runtime behavior unchanged.
- Use reinterpretation and visual/species clarity to reduce overlap.

## 1) `common-drift-pup`

### Current code-truth
- id: `common-drift-pup`
- imageKey: `common-drift-pup`
- name: `Drift Pup`
- tier: `common`
- affinity: `Explorer`
- habitat: `Zen Garden`
- shipZone: `zen`

### Identity problem
“Pup” overlaps with multiple canine-adjacent entries, and the current concept can read as a normal small dog with soft fantasy garnish.

### Locked species direction
A wind-seed tracker beastlet: a low-to-ground, glider-limbed companion whose body language is “testing routes and finding gentle forward motion.” Biological feel: mammal-meets-seed-skimmer.

### What it must NOT become
- Not a normal dog puppy.
- Not a wolf cub variant.
- Not a fox substitute.
- Not a generic fluffy quadruped.

### Silhouette rule
Boomerang-shoulder profile with sail-like forelimbs and a teardrop tail fin; instantly readable from side view.

### Movement inspiration
Sugar-glider launch + paper-plane drift + playful stop-start puppy hops (without literal dog gait dominance).

### Personality support role
Explorer support: gentle route-tester that reduces fear of starting and helps the player take the first imperfect step.

### Trait / weakness bridge
- Trait axis emphasis: Openness (safe experimentation), light Conscientiousness support (micro-commitment).
- Weakness bridge: `low_momentum`, `decision_confusion`.

### “Helps the player with ____” sentence
Helps the player with starting when the path feels fuzzy by turning “big beginning” into tiny safe trials.

### Visual motifs
- Seed-sail forelimb membranes
- Breeze-ripple fur tufts
- Teardrop fin-tail
- Pebble-scuff paws
- Pale windline markings

### Art prompt guardrails
Keep proportions compact and exploratory; emphasize glider-sail limbs and route-testing posture. Avoid realistic dog muzzle, breed-specific ears, or domestic puppy styling.

### Naming recommendation
Keep `Drift Pup` for now; consider future display-name evolution toward “Driftling” if overlap remains after art iteration.
