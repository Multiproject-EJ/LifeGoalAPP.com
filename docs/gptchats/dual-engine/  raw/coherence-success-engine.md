🧠 Who was David Bohm — and why you would care
David Bohm wasn’t just a physicist — he was a systems thinker about reality itself. If you’re building things like ValueBot, HabitGame, and your “wisdom OS,” Bohm is basically philosophy + physics + product design thinking merged into one person.

Here’s what would likely hit you:

1. Reality is not what it looks like (Implicate vs Explicate Order)
Bohm’s biggest idea:

The world we see = explicate order (surface-level reality)

Underneath = implicate order (hidden, deeper interconnected structure)

Think of it like:

UI (what users see)

vs

Backend system (what actually drives everything)

👉 He believed everything is fundamentally connected and unfolding from a deeper layer.

Why this matters for you:

Your apps are already about hidden structures shaping behavior (habits, psychology, markets)

Bohm would say: design the implicate layer, not just the UI

2. The universe is more like a flowing process than a thing
Bohm rejected the idea of static objects.

Instead:

Reality = continuous flow (he called it “holomovement”)

Everything is process, not object

This aligns perfectly with your thinking:

Habits → process

Investing → probabilistic unfolding

Personal growth → dynamic system

👉 Nothing is fixed. Everything is evolving patterns.

3. He challenged mainstream physics (and didn’t care about approval)
Bohm developed an alternative to quantum mechanics:

Bohmian Mechanics

Key idea:

Particles have real positions

But are guided by a hidden wave (pilot wave)

This was controversial because:

It brought back determinism + hidden structure

Most physicists preferred randomness

👉 This is very “you”:

You don’t accept surface-level explanations (markets, behavior)

You want the underlying mechanism

4. He worked with Albert Einstein — but went further
Einstein liked Bohm because:

He also believed reality should have deeper order

But Bohm went further:

He embraced wholeness, interconnectedness, and consciousness

Later, he even collaborated with:

Jiddu Krishnamurti

They explored:

Thought

Ego

Conditioning

Awareness

👉 This is where Bohm becomes extremely relevant to your “wisdom system”

5. Thought itself is broken (this is a big one for your app)
Bohm believed:

“Thought creates problems, then tries to solve them, without realizing it caused them.”

He argued:

Humans are trapped in self-reinforcing thought loops

We mistake thoughts for reality

Sound familiar?

👉 This is basically:

Negative mindset loops

Habit traps

“Stuck” users in your app

Your opportunity:

Your system could act as an external debugger for human thought

6. Dialogue > Debate (this is HUGE for your “cooperation over competition” idea)
Bohm created a concept called:

Bohm Dialogue

Rules:

No winning

No defending positions

Explore collectively

Let meaning emerge

👉 This is exactly aligned with your idea:

“big emphasis on cooperation and not destructive competition”

You could literally build:

A Bohm-style dialogue mode in your app

7. Fragmentation is the root problem
Bohm believed the biggest issue in society is:

Fragmentation (people, ideas, systems all split apart)

Examples:

Work vs life

Mind vs body

Individual vs society

👉 His solution:

Restore wholeness

Your apps are already doing this:

Life wheel = reintegration

Habit system = coherence

Investment model = structured reality

Better underlying order
