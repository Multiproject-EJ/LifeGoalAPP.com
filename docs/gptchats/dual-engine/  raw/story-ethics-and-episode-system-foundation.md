Small correction on your photon question: photons do not have quarks and gluons inside them in the Standard Model. Photons are treated as elementary particles. Protons and neutrons are composite: they are made of quarks bound by gluons. CERN describes ordinary matter as built from elementary particles, and notes that protons and neutrons are made of quarks held together by gluons. 

So what does “internal energy structure” of a proton mean? It means a proton is not just “three little balls glued together.” Yes, it has three valence quarks, but inside there is also a dynamic sea of gluons and quark–antiquark pairs carrying momentum and energy. CERN notes that in proton collisions it is really the quarks and gluons inside the proton that interact, and that much of the proton’s mass comes from the energy associated with gluons and the strong interaction, not just from the tiny bare masses of the quarks themselves.

Story mission
The story should entertain while gently weakening harmful certainty, increasing empathy, and making uncertainty feel playable rather than threatening.

Must do
respect the player

challenge certainty through consequences, not lectures

create identification with multiple characters

show that good intentions can still cause harm

keep hope alive

Must not do
mock religion / ideology directly

shame the player

preach in obvious moral language

flatten characters into villains

resolve everything with simplistic answers
